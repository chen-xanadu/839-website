On his way to California a doctor, <p>John</p> <p>Brighton</p>, decides to stay in Oklahoma territory after his wife dies in childbirth. He takes a room at the home of elderly Mrs. <p>Fitzgerald</p>, who helps raise his new daughter <p>Louise</p>.
Five years later, as he becomes acquainted with attractive widow <p>Anne</p> <p>Barnes</p> and her mother, Mrs. <p>Waynebrook</p>, the doctor treats the ill child of an Indian named <p>Charlie</p>. He also meets <p>Charlie</p>'s teen daughter, <p>Maria</p>, who is so good with children that he puts <p>Louise</p> in her care after Mrs. <p>Fitzgerald</p>'s death.
Wealthy rancher <p>Cass</p> <p>Dobie</p> and brother <p>Mel</p> are gobbling up land in the territory. When they determine that there is oil on <p>Charlie</p>'s property, they scheme to get it. <p>Mel</p> even tries to shoot <p>Charlie</p>, but is killed in self-defense. <p>John</p> testifies on <p>Charlie</p>'s behalf.
While awaiting a legal decision on the shooting, <p>Maria</p> professes her love for <p>John</p>. The angry <p>Cass</p> has a confrontation with <p>John</p>, who prevails, after which <p>Maria</p> realizes that <p>John</p> is actually in love with <p>Anne</p>.

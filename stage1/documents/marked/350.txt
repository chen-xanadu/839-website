As described in a film magazine,[3] <p>Moore</p> disgust at the hairpin-strewing, straggly locks of his young bride <p>Bennett</p> and her concentration upon extra-particularness in her housekeeping make it easy for him to forsake her company outside the home for that of his stenographer <p>Effie</p> <p>Wainwright</p>. Overhearing her husband's confession of her failure as a wife to him as he makes it to his employer, she considers suicide.

The story is of an English manufacturing town in which <p>Henry</p> <p>Little</p>, a worker and inventor, is persecuted by trade unions, jealous because he was better trained than his fellows. <p>Squire</p> <p>Raby</p>, <p>Little</p>'s uncle, is a forcible character, and a pleasant love story offsets the labor troubles. A purpose of the novel was to expose, without censure, the errors of early trades unions.

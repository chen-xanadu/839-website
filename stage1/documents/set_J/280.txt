<p>Rheinhardt</p>, a cynical drifter, gets a job as an announcer for a right-wing radio station, WUSA in New Orleans. <p>Rheinhardt</p> is content to parrot WUSA's reactionary editorial stance on the air, even if he does not agree with it. <p>Rheinhardt</p> finds his cynical detachment challenged by a woman he meets in a bar, <p>Geraldine</p>, and by <p>Rainey</p>, a neighbor and troubled idealist who becomes aware of WUSA's sinister, hidden purpose. And when events start spinning out of control, even <p>Rheinhardt</p> finds he must take a stand.[1]
<p>Bingamon</p>, the station's owner, is sponsoring a white-supremacist hate rally that draws a protest from black militants. <p>Rainey</p> attempts to assassinate <p>Bingamon</p>, but after he misses and accidentally wounds someone else, <p>Rainey</p> is attacked by the crowd and beaten to death. In the chaos, drugs end up in the possession of <p>Geraldine</p>, who is arrested by the police and later hangs herself in the jail cell. A disillusioned <p>Reinhardt</p> packs his bags and leaves town.

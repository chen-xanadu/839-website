When visiting San Francisco, <p>Tony</p> <p>Patucci</p>, an ageing illiterate winegrower from the Napa Valley, sees waitress <p>Amy</p> <p>Peters</p> and falls in love. Returning home, he persuades his foreman <p>Joe</p>, an incorrigible womanizer, to write her a letter in <p>Tony</p>'s name. <p>Tony</p>'s courtship by mail culminates with a proposal, and when she requests a picture of him, he sends one of <p>Joe</p>. <p>Amy</p> accepts and goes to Napa to be married. Although horrified to discover that her prospective husband is the portly <p>Tony</p>, she decides to go through with the marriage. However, while <p>Tony</p> is in bed after an accident, <p>Amy</p> and <p>Joe</p> have an affair. Two months later, as <p>Tony</p> plans the wedding, she discovers that she is pregnant. Upon learning this, <p>Tony</p> pummels <p>Joe</p>, who leaves the vineyards. but forgives <p>Amy</p>, and insists that they still be married, But she is unable to forgive herself, so she leaves with the priest who's come to marry them, while <p>Tony</p> looks on, hoping that she will return one day.

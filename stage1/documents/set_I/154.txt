Lieutenant <p>John</p> "<p>Junior</p>" <p>Witkowski</p> (<p>Bob</p> <p>Dornan</p>) and his buddy, Lieutenant <p>York</p> (<p>Steve</p> <p>Early</p>), arrive at George Air Force Base, Tactical Air Command, in Southern California to train to fly the Lockheed F-104 Starfighter, with special emphasis on the complicated mid-air refueling maneuver.
<p>Witkowski</p>'s congressman father (<p>Carl</p> <p>Rogers</p>), a famed World War II bomber pilot, frequently calls him, concerned about the safety of fighter aircraft. The congressman wants his son to be transferred to a Convair B-58 Hustler or Boeing B-52 Stratofortress bomber squadron in the Strategic Air Command. <p>Witkowski</p> also finds romance with <p>Mary</p> <p>Davidson</p> (<p>Shirley</p> <p>Olmstead</p>), an Iowa girl.
During training, <p>Major</p> <p>Stevens</p> (<p>Richard</p> <p>Jordahl</p>) sends <p>Witkowski</p>, York and <p>Lieutenant</p> <p>Lyons</p> (<p>Robert</p> <p>Winston</p>) on a cross-country mission. The three trainees are forced to separate as they encounter a storm. <p>Lyons</p>' aircraft goes down in the mountains, while <p>Witkowski</p> is feared lost. Later, they learn that <p>Lyons</p> parachuted safely, and <p>Witkowski</p> has landed safely at an alternate base. <p>Witkowski</p>, who has impressed senior officers and won his father's admiration, is among those selected to be transferred to a unit in Europe and bids a temporary good-bye to <p>Mary</p>.
